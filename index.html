Blast Freezing Principle

Pressure Differential Airflow

A high-volume evaporator fan wall creates a controlled pressure difference, forcing cold air through the product load for faster and more uniform freezing.

01

Build pressure difference

The fan wall creates a high-pressure supply side and a low-pressure return side.

02

Force air through product gaps

Air is guided through cartons, trays and pallet gaps instead of bypassing the load.

03

Remove heat from the product core

Continuous cold airflow reduces surface temperature and pulls heat from the center.

04

Recirculate through the coil

Warmer return air passes across the evaporator coil and is cooled again.

Customer Benefit

  • More uniform product temperature across the pallet.
  • Less air bypass around the outside of the load.
  • Faster passage through the maximum ice crystal formation zone.
  • Better suited for tunnel-style blast freezing rooms.
